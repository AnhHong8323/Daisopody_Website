01
First 48 hours · acclimate slowly

Open the box in a dim, ventilated room. Leave the shipping container half-open inside the prepared enclosure for 12 hours before releasing — temperature shock is the single biggest cause of first-week mortality.

02
Enclosure · 30 × 30 × 30 cm minimum

A four-layer bioactive build with cork bark hides. Calcium-rich substrate (we recommend our Bioactive blend). Heavy use of sphagnum moss in at least one humid corner.

03
Feeding · weekly + calcium top-up

Mixed leaf litter is the staple. Calcium twice a month via crushed cuttlebone. A single small protein source (fish food, dried shrimp) once every three weeks — not more.

04
Monitoring · the molting window

Expect the first major molt within 3 – 5 weeks of arrival. Pale, motionless specimens are mid-molt — leave them alone. Resume regular husbandry once the new exoskeleton hardens (24 – 36 h).
