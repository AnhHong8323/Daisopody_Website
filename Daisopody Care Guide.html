The questions we get most.

Pulled from our WhatsApp support thread — answered by our facility leads, on the record.

HUMIDITY · BEGINNER My substrate looks wet but the hygrometer reads 55% — which one do I trust?

Trust the hygrometer — but only after calibration. Cheap probes drift 8–12% within their first three months. Recalibrate using the salt-jar test (saturated salt + sealed container = 75% rH at 25°C), then mount the probe an inch above substrate level, not on the wall. Substrate appearance is a lagging indicator.

If the calibrated reading is still 55%, your enclosure is dry despite damp-looking substrate. Likely your ventilation is too aggressive — try halving the screen area before adding more water.
