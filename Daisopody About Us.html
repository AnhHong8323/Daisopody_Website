OUR STORY

It started with two cultures and a notebook.

In 2020, Linh — a microbiology graduate working in a Da Lat coffee co-op — bought ten Porcellio laevis from a Hanoi keeper. She wrote everything down: feed dates, molt observations, the relative humidity at 6 AM and 6 PM. When the colony reached its third generation she had a notebook full of patterns nobody had bothered to publish.

Marcus — her partner and an exotic-animal logistics nerd — saw what nobody else was building: a Vietnamese isopod operation with research-grade record-keeping. They turned the spare bedroom into Room 1, then converted the garage into Rooms 2 and 3 when the Cubaris lines began breeding reliably.

Today the same notebook system runs every culture we ship. Every shipped specimen carries a generation tag back to a founder pair we still keep cultures from.

WHAT WE STAND ON

Four principles we don't flex on.

01

Captive-bred, always.

No wild-caught specimens, ever. Every animal we sell has been raised through at least one full generation in our facility.

02

Pedigree on every shipment.

Each specimen ships with a signed lineage card traceable to founder generation. We never sell what we can't trace.

03

Data goes public.

Husbandry findings, mortality numbers, and humidity logs publish in our care library — quarterly, free, in two languages.

04

Support for life.

Buy one isopod from us, get unlimited keeper support on WhatsApp — for as long as that colony lives. No tiers, no premium plans.

THE FACILITY

Three rooms, four climate zones, one notebook.

Our Da Lat facility runs on a single record-keeping system that's stayed unchanged since 2021. Every culture in every room has the same kind of page in the same kind of binder.

Climate stabilisers hold each zone within ±0.5°C and ±3% rH around the clock. Substrate is blended on-site weekly. We never let a culture leave the facility without two consecutive weeks of healthy breeding logs.
